# Sunday – Rest & Comprehensive Recovery

## Sunday Recovery Protocol

### Active Recovery Options
- **Gentle yoga**: 30 minutes focusing on flexibility and relaxation
- **Leisure walk**: 30 minutes at easy pace, preferably outdoors

### Recovery Techniques
- **Extensive foam rolling**: Target all muscle groups from the week
- **Breathing exercises**: 15 minutes focused breathing techniques

## Recovery & Nutrition Week 5

- **Protein**: Maintain 1.8–2.2 g/kg body weight
- **Hydration**: 2–3 liters water daily with electrolytes
- **Sleep**: Consistent 8–9 hours nightly
- **Daily Monitoring**: Regular check-ins on soreness, fatigue, and HRV

## Quick Recovery Instructions

### Gentle Yoga (30 minutes)
- Focus on flexibility and relaxation
- Counter training-induced tightness
- Include hip and shoulder emphasis
- Deep breathing throughout
- Preparation for Week 6 intensification
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Mobility](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Leisure Walk (30 minutes)
- Easy outdoor pace
- Mental relaxation priority
- Active recovery from Development phase
- No fitness goals or intensity
- Enjoyment and stress relief

### Extensive Foam Rolling (20 minutes)
- Target all muscle groups from the week
- Extra attention to legs and back
- Prepare for upcoming power phase
- Gentle, thorough approach
- Include upper body and hips
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Recovery](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Breathing Exercises (15 minutes)
- Stress reduction and recovery
- Various breathing patterns
- Focus on nervous system recovery
- Prepare for Week 6 power training
- Mental and physical preparation

### Development Phase Assessment
- Review week's training adaptations
- Note areas of improvement
- Prepare mentally for power phase
- Plan nutrition and recovery
- Assess readiness for progression

## Key Focus Points

- **Complete Rest**: No structured training, focus on recovery
- **Gentle Movement**: Light activity to promote circulation
- **Recovery Optimization**: Foam rolling and breathing work
- **Preparation**: Ready for Week 6 continued Development phase
- **Monitoring**: Track adaptation to intensity ramp-up

## Notes

Careful monitoring and adjustments ensure optimal performance as training intensity escalates in the Development phase.
