## Saturday Options

### Option A: Match Day
- **Dynamic warm-up**: Progressive activation protocol for competition
- **Competitive focus**: Maximum intensity during match play
- **Thorough post-match stretching**: Comprehensive recovery protocol

### Option B: No Match Day
- **Zone-2 jog**: 30 minutes at conversational pace

## Quick Exercise Instructions

### Dynamic Warm-up (Match Day)
- 5 minutes general movement preparation
- 10 minutes progressive tennis-specific movements
- Mental preparation and tactical review
- Sport-specific activation patterns
- Competition readiness protocols
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Warm-up Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Zone-2 Jog (No Match Day)
- 30 minutes conversational pace
- Maintain aerobic base development
- Easy rhythm, no stress on body
- Active recovery from week's training
- Prepare for Week 4 deload
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Cardio Guidelines](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kl0ht3ow8TQ)

### Dynamic Stretching (15 minutes)
- Target all major muscle groups
- Hold dynamic movements 10-15 seconds
- Include tennis-specific patterns
- Address areas of tightness from training
- Prepare body for next week
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Mobility](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Post-Match Stretching
- Comprehensive recovery protocol
- 15-20 minutes static stretching
- Focus on maintaining flexibility
- Deep breathing for relaxation
- Promote next-day readiness
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Stretching Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

## Key Focus Points

- **Competition Preparation**: Dynamic warm-up protocols for optimal performance
- **Mental Focus**: Maintain competitive intensity throughout match
- **Recovery Protocol**: Immediate post-match care for next-day readiness
- **Active Recovery**: Maintain aerobic base with easy jogging
- **Mobility Work**: Dynamic stretching addressing week's training stress

## Notes

Saturday provides competition experience or active recovery, both supporting the training cycle's progression toward Week 4 deload.
