## Quick Exercise Instructions

### Easy Tennis Agility Drills
- Moderate intensity, controlled pace
- Focus on movement quality over speed
- Practice technique without high intensity
- Maintain good form throughout
- Use as active recovery movement

### Trap-bar Deadlift (3×6)
- Moderate weight (reduce from Week 3)
- Focus on perfect form and technique
- Smooth controlled movement
- Hip hinge pattern maintenance
- Preparation for Week 5 progression
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Deadlift Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=op9kVnSso6Q)

### Hip Thrusts (2×8)
- Gentle reps with reduced volume
- Focus on movement quality
- Control both up and down phases
- Light to moderate resistance
- Maintain proper form
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Hip Thrust Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xDmFkJxPzeM)

### DB Shoulder Press (2×8)
- Light weight with stable core
- Focus on shoulder health
- Full range of motion
- Control both directions
- Maintain perfect posture
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Shoulder Training](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8_7BoZrCPco)

### Farmer's Walk (2×30m)
- Moderate weight, controlled pace
- Shorter distance than previous weeks
- Focus on posture and form
- Gentle grip challenge
- Active recovery movement
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Farmer's Walk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p8FWv6bUJ7w)

### Gentle Plyometrics (2×5)
- Soft landings, minimal intensity
- Focus on landing mechanics
- Reduced volume and intensity
- Movement pattern practice
- Prepare for future progressions
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Plyometric Training](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tcY1dI5-Teo)

## Key Focus Points (Deload Week)

- **Easy Agility**: Moderate intensity with controlled pace
- **Moderate Loads**: Reduced weights across all exercises
- **Gentle Plyometrics**: Soft landings with minimal intensity
- **Form Focus**: Emphasis on technique over load

## Notes

Friday maintains movement patterns with significantly reduced loads, preparing for Week 5 progression.
