# Sunday – Rest & Comprehensive Recovery

## Sunday Recovery Protocol (Deload Week)

### Complete Rest Activities
- **Gentle yoga**: 30 minutes restorative practice
- **Leisure walk**: 30 minutes easy pace
- **Extended foam rolling**: Target all muscle groups
- **Breathwork exercises**: 15 minutes stress reduction

## Recovery & Nutrition Week 4 (Deload)
- **Protein**: Maintain at 1.8–2.2 g/kg body weight
- **Hydration**: Continue 2–3 liters water daily, electrolytes
- **Sleep**: Prioritize 8–9 hours per night
- **Monitoring**: Close attention to recovery markers

## Quick Recovery Instructions (Deload Week)

### Restorative Yoga (30 minutes)
- Extended restorative practice
- Hold poses 60-90 seconds
- Focus on deep relaxation
- Include supported poses with props
- End with long final relaxation
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Mobility](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Extended Walk (30 minutes)
- Longer, leisurely walk
- Focus on mental relaxation
- Enjoy nature and fresh air
- No time pressure or goals
- Mental break from training stress

### Comprehensive Foam Rolling
- Extended session (25-30 minutes)
- Target entire body thoroughly
- Gentle pressure, focus on relaxation
- Include neglected areas
- Full recovery emphasis
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Recovery](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Extended Breathwork (15 minutes)
- Longer stress reduction session
- Multiple breathing techniques
- Focus on complete relaxation
- Nervous system restoration
- Prepare for Development phase

### Sleep Optimization Focus
- Plan for 8-9 hours sleep
- Optimize sleep environment
- No screens before bed
- Focus on sleep quality
- Full recovery priority

## Key Focus Points (Deload Week)

- **Complete Recovery**: Focus on full nervous system restoration
- **Sleep Optimization**: Extended sleep for adaptation
- **Stress Reduction**: Mental and physical relaxation techniques
- **Preparation**: Ready for Week 5 Development phase

## Notes

Deload week is critical for full recovery and adaptation, setting the stage for intensified training in upcoming Development phase.
