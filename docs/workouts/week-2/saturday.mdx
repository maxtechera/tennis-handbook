## Saturday Options

### Option A: Match Day
- **Structured warm-up**: 15-20 minutes progressive activation
- **High focus during play**: Maximum competitive intensity
- **Thorough cool-down stretching**: 10-15 minutes comprehensive stretching

### Option B: No Match Day
- **Zone-2 jog**: 30 minutes at conversational pace
- **Dynamic mobility**: 20 minutes targeting all major joints

## Quick Exercise Instructions

### Structured Warm-up (Match Day)
- 5 minutes light movement (jogging, dynamic stretching)
- 5 minutes sport-specific movements (shadow tennis strokes)
- 5-10 minutes progressive tennis hitting
- Mental preparation and game plan review
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Warm-up Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Zone-2 Jog (No Match Day)
- 30 minutes at conversational pace
- Maintain 60-70% maximum heart rate
- Able to hold conversation throughout
- Focus on easy, sustainable rhythm
- Active recovery for training adaptation
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Cardio Guidelines](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kl0ht3ow8TQ)

### Dynamic Mobility (20 minutes)
- Leg swings (10 each direction)
- Hip circles (10 each direction)
- Arm circles and shoulder rolls
- Torso twists and side bends
- Focus on major joints used in tennis
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Mobility](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

### Cool-down Stretching (Match Day)
- Hold each stretch 30-60 seconds
- Focus on hip flexors, hamstrings, calves
- Include shoulder and chest stretches
- Deep breathing during holds
- Promote recovery for next day
- **Video Reference**: [Jeff Nippard - Stretching Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8wPpK6xW9s8)

## Key Focus Points

- **Match Preparation**: If competing, focus on proper warm-up protocols
- **Competitive Mindset**: Maintain high focus and intensity during match play
- **Recovery Protocol**: Thorough cool-down to aid next-day recovery
- **Active Recovery**: If no match, maintain movement with easy aerobic work
- **Mobility Work**: Dynamic mobility addressing any tight areas from the week

## Notes

Saturday provides match experience or active recovery depending on schedule. Both options support the training cycle appropriately.
